Spell Checker Flags Same Word as Misspelled With Revisions

 Last reviewed: February 5, 1998
Article ID: Q155465
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Word for Windows 95, versions 7.0, 7.0a

SYMPTOMS

When you spell check a document that has revisions, the speller replaces an incorrect word with a correctly spelled one, but then identifies the newly inserted word as misspelled.

CAUSE

You highlighted a portion of a word and replaced it with another word. The speller will detect and replace the misspelled word with a correctly spelled one but the newly corrected word, in combination with the portion of the revised word that is struck-through, creates another misspelled word. This is why the spell checker remains on the screen; it has detected that this new combination of letters creates another misspelled word.

STATUS

Microsoft has confirmed this to be a problem in the products listed at the beginning of this article. Microsoft is researching this problem and will post new information here in the Microsoft Knowledge Base as it becomes available.

WORKAROUND

Select entire words, instead of portions of words, to revise.
