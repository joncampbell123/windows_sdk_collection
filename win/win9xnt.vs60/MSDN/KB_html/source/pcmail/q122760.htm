PC Win: Shared Folders View Cannot Be Expanded

 Last reviewed: December 13, 1994
Article ID: Q122760
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Mail for Windows, versions 3.2

In version 3.2 of Microsoft Mail for Windows, the private folders can be expanded or collapsed. When the folders are fully expanded, the parent level folders show a minus sign (-), indicating they can be collapsed. When collapsed, the symbol turns to a plus sign (+).

Shared folders in Mail for Windows cannot be manipulated like the private folders because the shared folder hierarchy can be changed by any of the Mail for PC Networks workstations (Mail for PC Networks, Macintosh workstation; Mail for PC Networks, OS/2 workstation; or Mail for PC Networks, MS-DOS workstation). Because there was no easy way to track the expanded status of the folders at the client level, it was decided not to be included in any future releases or enhancements of version 3.2x of Mail for Windows.
