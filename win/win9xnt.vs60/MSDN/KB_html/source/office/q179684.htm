OFF98: Fonts Appear in the Trash After Installing Office 98

 Last reviewed: March 9, 1998
Article ID: Q179684
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition

SYMPTOMS

After you install Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition on your computer, the following files may appear in the Trash: 

   Arial
   Times New Roman
   Wingdings
   Microsoft Office Manager
NOTE: Other fonts may also appear in the Trash. You are not notified during installation that the files are being moved to the Trash.

CAUSE

Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition includes new versions of the Arial, Times New Roman, and Wingdings fonts, and new versions of some less commonly used fonts.

If older versions of these fonts are found on the computer when you install Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition, the old fonts are moved to the Trash and are replaced by the new fonts.

If you have Microsoft Office Manager from Microsoft Office 4.2 and you run the Office 98 Installer, a new copy of the Microsoft Office Manager is copied to your Control Panels folder. The old copy is moved to the Trash and you have to manually empty the Trash to get rid of it.

The Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition installation program is not designed to remove the old files from the Trash after the installation process is completed. As a result, the old files appear in the Trash. This may lead you to believe that the fonts and Microsoft Office Manager are not available for use on your computer.

RESOLUTION

If this behavior occurs, empty the Trash by clicking Empty Trash on the Special menu.

STATUS

This behavior is by design of the Microsoft Office 98 Macintosh Edition installation program.
