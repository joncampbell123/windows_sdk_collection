Excel: Maximum Number of Characters in a Formula Is Incorrect

 Last reviewed: November 3, 1994
Article ID: Q75810
Summary:

Page 127 of the "Microsoft Excel User's Guide" version 3.0 for the Macintosh manual incorrectly states that "Microsoft Excel accepts formulas up to 4,096 characters long." A formula is limited by 1024 bytes of information, not characters. Depending on the complexity, your formula may be as many as 300 characters. Text strings are limited to 255 characters.

More Information:

For more information on the limitations of a formula's length, query on the following words: 

   formula and complexity
