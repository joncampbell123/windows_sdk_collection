OL97: Monthly Calendar Printing Limitations

 Last reviewed: January 19, 1998
Article ID: Q163668
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Outlook 97

SYMPTOMS

When you print a series of monthly calendars in Microsoft Outlook 97, the printout does not have page breaks at the beginning of each month.

RESOLUTION

To assure that the printout has page breaks at the beginning of each month in a series, print one month at a time. To do this, follow these steps:

  1. From the Calendar view in Outlook, click the Print icon on the toolbar.

  2. In the Print dialog box, under Print Style, click Monthly Style.

  3. Under Print range, in the Start box, click the arrow, and on the drop-down calendar, click the first day of the month you want to print.

  4. Under Print range, in the End box, click the arrow, and on the drop-down calendar, click the last day of the month you want to print.

  5. Click OK.

Repeat these steps for EACH month in the series you want to print.

NOTE: Even when you specify Start and End dates, the calendar printout will reflect five full weeks. Part of the previous month and/or part of the next month may be displayed in the printout as well. Also, the monthly calendar printout in Outlook is limited to five full weeks. If a given month overlaps more than five weeks, part of the month will not be displayed in the printout. See March 1997 as an example.

MORE INFORMATION

Steps to Reproduce Behavior

  1. From the Calendar view in Outlook, click the Print icon on the toolbar.

  2. In the Print dialog box, under Print Style, click Monthly Style.

  3. Under Print range, in the Start box, click the arrow, and on the drop-down calendar, click the first day of the first month in the series you want to print.

  4. Under Print range, in the End box, click the arrow, and on the drop-down calendar, click the last day of the last month in the series you want to print.

  5. Click OK.

If for instance, you chose to print January through April of 1997, the result would display January on the first page, February and March on the second page, March and April on the third page, and April and May on the fourth page.
