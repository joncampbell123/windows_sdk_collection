OL97: Some Manual Journal Entries Are Not Logged to Contacts

 Last reviewed: January 19, 1998
Article ID: Q165411
The information in this article applies to:
  • Microsoft Outlook 97

SYMPTOMS

In Outlook 97, a manual Journal entry may not be logged to the Contact's Journal page.

CAUSE

You started the entry from the Journal folder with a New Journal entry, and used the Address Book to retrieve the Contact's name for the Journal entry.

WORKAROUND

To log a manual Journal entry to a Contact, you must either initiate the manual entry from the open Contact record, or select the Contact's name from the Global or Pesonal Address Book.

Method 1

To start the manual entry from the open Contact, do the following:

  1. Open the Contact record.

  2. Click the Journal tab.

  3. Click New Journal Entry and enter the desired information.

  4. Click Save and then Close.

RESULT: The entry will be logged properly.

Method 2

To start the manual entry from the Journal folder, do the following:

  1. In the Journal folder, click the New Journal button.

  2. Type an entry on the Subject line.

  3. Click Address Book next to the Contact line.

  4. Select the Contact name from the Global Address Book or the Personal Address Book. Or type in the name and resolve it by clicking Check Names.

  5. Click Save and then Close.

  6. In the Contacts folder, click the Contact chosen in step 4 above.

  7. Click the Journal tab.

RESULT: The entry is logged to the Contact as expected.

MORE INFORMATION

Steps to Reproduce the Problem

  1. In the Journal folder, click New Journal.

  2. Type an entry on the Subject line.

  3. Click Address Book next to the Contact line.

  4. In the "Show Names from the" list, click Contacts under Outlook Address Book.

  5. Click a Contact on the list and click Add and then OK.

  6. Click Save and then Close.

  7. In the Contacts folder, click the Contact chosen in step 5 above.

  8. Click the Journal tab.

RESULT: The journal entry is NOT logged to the Contact's Journal page.
